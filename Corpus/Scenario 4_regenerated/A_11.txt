Toward the southern sea lies Surfmist Bay, a coastal stretch flanked by limestone cliffs that gleam under midday sun. Fishing villages perch precariously atop these cliffs, their whitewashed cottages contrasting azure waters below. Dawn watchers line bluff trails, capturing first glimpses of the sun emerging over tidal flats. A winding staircase descends to hidden coves where starfish cling to rocks, revealed by each receding wave. Salty breezes toss lines of laundry strung between cottages, while swirling gulls circle in search of scraps from morning catches. Traps brimming with crabs rest on battered wharves, overseen by grizzled sailors sharing briny anecdotes. The midday lull sees children sliding down sandy dunes to collect driftwood or decorative shells. Occasionally, a lone bagpiper practices tunes that echo hauntingly across the cove. As dusk nears, lovers gather on cliff edges to watch the sun melt into shimmering horizons, painting the sky in streaks of tangerine and rose. A battered old church stands sentinel on a rocky rise, its bells marking shifting tides with measured tolls. Nights bring bonfires on the beach, sparks rising toward starlit skies while neighbors pass around grilled fish and tangy lemon sauce.
