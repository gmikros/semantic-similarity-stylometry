Hidden inland from Surfmist Bay, the orchard-laced realm of Applegrove brims with winding roads flanked by apple trees heavy with crimson fruit. White blossoms drift in late spring, carpeting grassy verges like confetti. At daybreak, orchard workers climb wooden ladders to gather sun-kissed apples, their laughter echoing across dewy rows. A quaint press house stands near an ancient barn, producing sweet cider revered by locals and visitors alike. Honeybees flit among blossoms, ensuring each orchard yields a plentiful harvest. By midday, picnics unfold under shady boughs where grandparents tell children about the orchard’s founding centuries ago. Wooden barrels line rutted paths, awaiting the tangy liquid that ferments into cherished spirits come autumn. Early afternoons often see blacksmiths clanging iron in a tiny forge across the orchard lane, forging horseshoes for passing wagons. Soft breezes rustle orchard leaves in a gentle lullaby, luring passersby into peaceful naps. A solitary chapel’s bells ring out at dusk, signaling orchard hands to finish sorting and retire. Rustic cabins perched on nearby slopes glow from within, where families gather around wooden tables laden with pies, preserves, and spiced teas. The orchard floor glistens beneath moonlight, revealing scurrying woodland creatures in search of fallen apples. Seasonal festivals bring strings of fairy lights and fiddlers who serenade dancing revelers among rows of blossoming branches.
