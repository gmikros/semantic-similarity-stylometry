North of Glenlowen lies the fabled region of Windstoke, famed for its boundless fields of heather that ripple beneath constant breezes. Narrow farm roads wind between rolling pastures where sleek horses graze under open skies. By dawn, the horizon glows with pastel clouds, while windmills scattered among the hills harness the gusts that define local life. Roadside stands sell jars of honey from bees that frequent lavender rows near centuries-old barns. Shepherd dogs herd docile sheep across quiet lanes, their muffled bleats carried away on strong drafts. Afternoon finds village greens bustling with artisans weaving wool or shaping pottery at spinning wheels. Songbirds ride the wind’s currents, their chirps weaving into a natural lullaby. Locals joke that Windstoke’s breezes can snatch hats and secrets alike, depositing them miles away. A solitary chapel anchors the largest hamlet, its weather vane forever squeaking in the restless air. Even when thunderheads loom, farmers hum old ballads, confident that storms replenish their meadows. At dusk, brilliant saffron sunsets melt into silhouettes of watchful barns and tall windmills cutting across orange skies. Children gather near a wind-bent oak, threading daisies into crowns while grown-ups trade news about harvest yields. Rumors swirl of roving fiddlers who appear unannounced, playing lively jigs that echo for miles. Visitors might awaken to find mild gales rattling shutters, yet comfort lingers in the warm kitchens offering jam-smothered scones.
